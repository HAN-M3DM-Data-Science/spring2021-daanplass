According to (Andersson, 2016); there are four charecteristics that define a data driven organisation. 1. Data is centralized and organized. 2. Data governance policies are in place. 3. Data is accessible. 4. Analytics is integrated into tools. Data is as imporant as other parts of the business and has an open environment/community that encourages growth and learning. (With data is meant the entire department that continuesly works on data improvement etc. such as data scientists, analysists, software engineers, etc.) ## What are the important features of a data culture? According to ("4 Characteristics of a Data-Driven Culture," 2020); there are four characteristics that define a date culture. 1. Democratized data. 2. Data literacy. (Piyanka Jain) "the ability to read and use data to draw a meaningful conclusion to power decisions." 3. Data-driven leadership. Creating a infrastructure that supports data-driven processes, leaders must build a open culture where people are comfortable to explore new technologies and propose their own data supported hypotheses. 4. Decision-making process. ## What are the transformation steps towards a mature data driven business? Learn what is going on in the market, investigate best practices externally. Investigate how the technologies, culture and standards will be affected by step of becoming a data driven business. Setting up KPIs to identify which data is essential and will create value to the company. Making data as important as other parts of the business.
